The capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is a giant cavy rodent commonly known by many names. In fact, it is the largest living rodent both in size and weight. Its close relatives include guinea pigs and rock cavies, and it is more distantly related to the agouti, the chinchilla, and the nutria. The capybara inhabits savannas and dense forests and lives near bodies of water. It is a highly social species and can be found in groups as large as 100 individuals, but usually lives in groups of 10-20 individuals.
