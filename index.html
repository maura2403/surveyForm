The capybara or carpincho (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is a giant cavy rodent commonly known by many names. In fact, it is the largest living rodent in both size and weight. Its close relatives include guinea pigs and rock cavies, and it is more distantly related to the agouti, the chinchilla, and the nutria. It is a highly social species and can be found in groups as large as 100 individuals, but usually lives in groups of 10-20 individuals.
